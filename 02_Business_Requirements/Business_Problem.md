# Business Problem

## Background

Merchology used NetSuite as its ERP and ShipHawk as its Warehouse Management System (WMS) and Transportation Management System (TMS). Sales orders were created and maintained in NetSuite and synchronized to ShipHawk for warehouse processing.

## Problem Statement

Inventory-related changes made in NetSuite after an order had already been transferred to ShipHawk were not automatically synchronized.

As a result, ShipHawk continued processing outdated order information while NetSuite contained the latest version of the order.

The mismatch was typically discovered by the Receiving Team during inventory receipt, causing fulfillment delays and requiring manual intervention from warehouse leads or supervisors.

## Business Impact

- Delays in warehouse receiving
- Manual intervention by Leads/Supervisors
- Increased operational effort
- Fulfillment delays
- Risk of inventory discrepancies
- Reduced operational efficiency during peak periods

## Estimated Volume

- Approximately 20 affected orders per day during normal operations
- Up to 50 affected orders per day during peak season

## Goal

Identify inventory synchronization exceptions as early as possible, reduce manual effort, improve warehouse efficiency, and ensure NetSuite and ShipHawk remain synchronized for inventory-related changes.
